How to prevent Patellofemoral Syndrome

Maintaining a healthy weight.
Wearing appropriate shoes for your activities.
Stretching and warming up before activity.
Increasing athletic training intensity gradually.
Avoiding activities that hurt your knee in the past.

how to prevent Shoulder Injury

Listen to your body. If your shoulder gets sore after any activity, don't ignore it. If the pain is serious and doesn't go away, see your doctor. ... Stay in shape. Keep your body in good physical shape with regular exercise and a healthy diet. ... Exercise the right way. Warm up before you work out.

how to prevent Tennis or Golf Elbow.

Strengthen your forearm muscles. Use light weights or squeeze a tennis ball.
Stretch before your activity. Walk or jog for a few minutes to warm up your muscles.
Fix your form.
Use the right equipment.
Lift properly.
Know when to rest.

how to prevent Hamstring Strain.

Warm up properly before exercise or intense physical activity.
Keep your muscles strong and flexible year-round.
Increase the duration and intensity of your exercise slowly.
If you feel pain in your thigh, stop your activity immediately.

how to prevent Shin Splints

Stretch your calves and hamstrings.
Avoid sudden increases in physical activity.
Exercise on softer surfaces when possible.
Strengthen your foot and the arch of your foot.
Strengthen your hip muscles.
Buy new athletic shoes that are right for you.
Stay at a healthy body weight.

how to prevent Groin Pull

Ice the area. Apply ice to the injured area as soon as possible to help limit the swelling
The severity of your groin injury determines how long you need to refrain from physical activity
Compress the injured groin muscle. Compression helps to further reduce swelling and stabilize.

How to prevent Concussion

Everyone should wear properly fitting, sport-appropriate headgear and safety equipment when playing contact sports or biking, rollerblading, skateboarding
, snowboarding, or skiing. You can't prevent every concussion. But helmets,
mouthguards, and other safety gear can reduce the risk of a brain injury.

How to prevent colles fracture

Nutrition. Make sure you're getting enough calcium by eating dark, leafy green vegetables and low-fat dairy products fortified with calcium.
Exercise. Exercise helps strengthen your bones and muscles, which makes you less likely to fall and fracture a bone.
Wrist guard.

How to prevent Sprained ankles

Stretch regularly to keep your ankles flexible.
Do ankle range of motion and strengthening exercises to keep your muscles strong.
Always warm up before playing sports, exercising, or doing any other kind of physical activity

How to prevent Strained backs

Do back-strengthening and stretching exercises at least 2 days a week
Stand and sit up straight.
Avoid heavy lifting. If you do lift something heavy, bend your knees and keep your back straight.
Get active and eat healthy. Being overweight can strain your back.
